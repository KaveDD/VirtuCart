Project Scope

Literature Survey

In the present times as consumerism has grown, supermarkets have become the ideal place for a consumer to purchase their daily necessities, groceries, and other household items. Given the density and choice of products that have increased with time, customers can find the task of locating and selecting items in a supermarket to be overwhelming, time-consuming, and complicated. According to a study on the intersection of technology and marketing and How Technology is Changing Consumer Expectations and Marketing Strategies in the Data-Driven Culture, it has been found that in today's data-driven culture, customers expect personalized services from merchants in exchange for sharing their consumer insights and data [1] A customer who enters the supermarket is most often aiming to get to the store, pick up the necessities and leave as fast as possible to get back to their busy lives — but what happens most of the time is that they end up wasting time walking around looking for items in their shopping list in a crowded store. Due to that, they might miss deals and opportunities. The conventional reliance on human shop assistants for guidance, having to go through one by one to find their health and nutrition factors in relevance to the customer and having to find deals and discounts themselves can all be solved by an automated system. People may face difficulties when trying to check the availability of products at a supermarket before visiting. Supermarkets may not have real-time data on their product inventory, leading to inaccuracies in online or phone-based product searches. Manual systems put pressure on people to be correct in all details of their work at all times, the problem being that people aren’t perfect. The use of augmented reality (AR) in retail settings has grown significantly in recent years, due to technological advancements. AR technology enables 3D objects to be placed in real-world environments, complementing or superimposing computer-generated virtual objects onto real world objects [2]. By adopting this approach, it becomes possible to combine physical and virtual objects within the same space, thereby maintaining the significance of human interaction while maximizing efficiency. Although augmented and mixed reality are relatively new technologies, they have been utilized for similar purposes by various global brands. IKEA has a VR (Virtual Reality) app that allows users to traverse the virtual showroom and visualize the furniture pieces in their own spaces before purchasing. Lowe’s, an American retail company developed a VR tool that allows users to design their home improvements. Alibaba introduced Buy+ to allow customers to browse and purchase products within a VR environment. We have yet to see a similar application by a local brand let alone a supermarket. The VirtuCart application aims to solve these problems that arise when shopping traditionally and then some as well as the ability to take mixed reality a step further locally.

    • Research Problem & Solution

    Proposed Problem

    Are you tired of wasting time in crowded supermarkets, missing out on deals, and struggling to find health-conscious options while juggling your busy life?

    In the realm of supermarket shopping, the common scenario of entering with the intention to swiftly gather necessities and leave promptly often turns into a time-consuming ordeal. Consumers find themselves navigating crowded aisles, missing out on potential savings, and struggling to locate health-conscious options amidst a sea of products. This is primarily due to the absence of integrated systems within stores that can effectively collect and analyze consumer data for personalized recommendations and exclusive deals. Additionally, checking product availability before visiting supermarkets is challenging due to a lack of real-time inventory data. Traditional manual systems place undue pressure on consumers to maintain absolute precision in their choices, which is often an unrealistic expectation. Therefore, there is a pressing need to enhance the retail shopping experience by integrating accurate, innovative, and digitally enhanced methods, particularly in Sri Lanka, where current solutions often fall short in terms of precision.

    Proposed Solution

    Introduce VirtuCart futuristic shopping experience

    In this innovative system, smartphones are connected to the internet, enabling access to an AR-based navigation system, which, in turn, connects to a database housing supermarket information. Integrated with this system is a virtual assistant that interacts with customers and is linked to a recommendation engine using customer data to suggest personalized products and deals. An AR item detection system is linked to the payment system, allowing real-time bill generation, with a cart capable of measuring weight. To ensure data consistency, the item detection system and cart are synchronized. The virtual assistant provides real-time product, deal, and promotion information on the customer's smartphone through an AR interface. Additionally, the system collects data on customer behavior and preferences to enhance the navigation and recommendation systems. Finally, at checkout, cashiers verify the bill, cart weight, and collect payment.

    Research Objectives

    AR Navigation for Supermarkets

    To design and develop a novel supermarket navigation system that uses a AR location scaning mehtod usinng LiDAR sensor. To enhance the overall shopping experience of customers by providing a more convenient and seamless navigation system.

    Weight Measuring Shopping Cart

    Develop a weight measuring shopping cart that accurately measures the weight of items added to the cart. Conduct thorough testing of the system to ensure that it meets all requirements and works as expected.

    Hand Wrriting Recognition

    Use Hand Writing Recognition system for scan the shopping list by capturing it using phone camera or upload necessary image of the shopping list which is saved in phone gallery.

    Product Identification and Display Information

    Collect and identify health and nutrition requirements unique to the customer relevant to product being purchased and Item detection using AR technology

    Smart Virtual Assistant

    Increase sales by suggesting complementary products and offering promotions and discounts to customers through the smart virtual assistant.

    Methodology

    The diagram presents a novel strategy for improving the experience of grocery shopping. Customers can easily browse the store with the help of an augmented reality-based navigation system and a mobile app. The solution ensures quick access to essential store information to link the customer's smartphone to the AR-based navigation system. Handwritten shopping lists are easily converted to digital photos, making it easier to navigate through an extensive database that holds product specifications, layout specifics, and exclusive offers. A virtual assistant that provides speech-based communication enhances the client experience even more. The recommendation engine and virtual assistant work together closely to customize product recommendations based on user preferences and previous purchases. Item detection makes real-time invoicing easier, and a weight-measuring cart ensures data accuracy. The system makes sure that the cart matches the weight at the conclusion of the procedure to avoid any discrepancies. Through an AR interface on their smartphones, customers may access real-time information on product locations, deals, and promotions. Additionally, the system collects user behavior and preference data, which helps to continuously enhance the AR-based navigation and recommendation engine. Cashiers double-check the amount and cart weight at the register to guarantee safe payment methods, including internet transactions. This approach improves convenience and customization while redefining the grocery shopping experience.
